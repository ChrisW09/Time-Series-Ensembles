Trend and Seasonality:

To introduce more patterns, consider adding trend and seasonality components.
For trend, you can use a linear or polynomial trend. For example:

Linear trend: (y_t = \beta_0 + \beta_1 t + \epsilon_t)
Quadratic trend: (y_t = \beta_0 + \beta_1 t + \beta_2 t^2 + \epsilon_t)


For seasonality, you can add periodic patterns (e.g., daily, weekly, monthly). For example:

Weekly seasonality: (y_t = y_{t-7} + \epsilon_t)
Monthly seasonality: (y_t = y_{t-30} + \epsilon_t)





Autoregressive (AR) and Moving Average (MA) Components:

Extend your time series by incorporating autoregressive (AR) and moving average (MA) components.
AR(p) process: (y_t = \phi_0 + \phi_1 y_{t-1} + \phi_2 y_{t-2} + \ldots + \phi_p y_{t-p} + \epsilon_t)
MA(q) process: (y_t = \theta_0 + \theta_1 \epsilon_{t-1} + \theta_2 \epsilon_{t-2} + \ldots + \theta_q \epsilon_{t-q} + \epsilon_t)
Adjust the coefficients (\phi_i) and (\theta_i) to control the persistence and smoothness of the time series.



Seasonal ARIMA (SARIMA):

Combine the above components to create a more complex SARIMA process.
SARIMA(p, d, q)(P, D, Q, s) includes seasonal differencing, autoregressive terms, moving average terms, and seasonal components.
Experiment with different orders (p, d, q, P, D, Q) to capture the desired patterns.
